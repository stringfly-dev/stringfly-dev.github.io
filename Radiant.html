Radiant

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Radiant is a gamer reward platform that monetizes moments across Web2 and Web3 games, turning into passive income through gamification and tokenized gaming data. It continuously captures gameplay signals, creating actionable context for AI, while making play rewarding beyond the game.

THE CHALLENGE

I worked on the Player Profile and reward experience, focusing on level progression, reward flows, and gamified feedback, working closely with the Game Design team to translate game mechanics into clear, engaging UX and UI.

DESIGN APPROACH

Level-Up Flow

I collaborated closely with the PO and Game Design team to translate level-up mechanics and quest rewards into an immersive user experience. When users achieve a quest or level up, a full-screen overlay appears, designed with motion and interaction to celebrate progression and make rewards feel meaningful.


Level Guide

I designed a Level Guide that clearly explains what users can unlock at each level, including rewards, abilities, and slots. The interface highlights the current level, next level, and upcoming unlocks, helping users understand their progression at a glance.

I also designed reward and ability unlock components to clearly communicate the type and value of each reward, helping users immediately understand what they‚Äôve unlocked.

Player Profile

I redesigned the Player Profile to make progression and status more transparent. The updated profile clearly shows level progress, how much XP is needed to reach the next level, and which rewards will be unlocked next. giving players a stronger sense of direction and motivation.

OUTCOMES & FEEDBACK

  • The immersive level-up overlay increased the perceived value of rewards and achievements.
  • The redesigned Player Profile gave users a clearer sense of status, goals, and next rewards.

KEY LEARNINGS & REFLECTIONS

  • I learned how to design UX that supports game mechanics without overwhelming players with information.
  • This project deepened my understanding of progression systems, reward psychology, and motivation-driven design.
